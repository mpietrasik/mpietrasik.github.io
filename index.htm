Biography

I am a final year PhD student in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Alberta working under the supervision of Prof. Marek Reformat. My research area lies at the intersection of knowledge graphs and artificial intelligence. Specifically, I am interested in automated ways of learning and extracting structures which are implicit in graph data. In the past, I have worked on methods to induce hierarchies from knowledge graphs as well as probabilistic ways of modelling social networks. Currently, I am looking at ways of generalizing knowledge graphs into abstract concepts.

Below are keywords of research topics which are of interest to me:

artificial intelligence, deep learning, knowledge graphs, machine learning, probabilistic graphical models, semantic web, statistical inference
